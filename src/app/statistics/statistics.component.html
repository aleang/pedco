We left New Zealand on the 17th of March 2018, taking with us two trusty bicycles to China. We landed the next day and assembled our gears at the airport. After several hours we set off. By the seventh of December 2019 we reached our final destination Lisbon, Portugal. As it was coming to winter, we hurried to finish just in time to celebrate Christmas and New Years in the UK.

The calculation is based on a 5-days-per-week routine. There are two exceptions (Bulgaria and Bosnia-Herzegovina) where we waited for several weeks to get our UK visa. Our general average was between 50-70 km a day. Unfortunately, this chart doesn't show the amount of weight we carried, the passes we had to climb, the detours we had to take, etc. The furthest we got in one day was 170 km.

The three outliers (China, Iran and Turkey) were countries we spent most of our time in. Apart from Iran, our general cycling direction was westward. We tried to make the most direct route possible, but occasionally take a different turn to visit something worthwhile. Monaco isn't on here but take the record for the shortest distance cycled (less than 1 km).
